Dunboyne is a small village in County Meath, Ireland.

Dunboyne's history dates back to the 1700's, with many of its men fighting in the 1798 rebellion against British Rule.

Dunboyne Castle is a building which was originally built in the 1800s for the Lord of Dunboyne. It was then turned into a convent which closed in 1991, and then sold and converted into a hotel in 2006.

The population is currently about 12,000, with this number expanding every year due to its repuation as a nice family area with good amenities and local schools.

Dunboyne is home to the longest running Motor Show in Ireland, with a festival held in October of every year celebrating old cars which have retained their engineering beauty.
